Notes

Parametric methods are the type of methods that assume a certain shape of a distribution. For example, the following equation is used to calculate the confidence interval of a mean of a distribution:

CI of mean = sample mean $\pm$ $($distribution score $\times$ Standard Error $)$

The variable in the equation, distribution score, depends on the type of the distribution. If you do not know the distribution shape of your data, it is very difficult to obtain the value of the distribution score. On the other hand, non-parametric methods do not assume anything about a distribution. A non-parametric alternative to calculate confidencer interval of mean is to use Bootstrapping.
